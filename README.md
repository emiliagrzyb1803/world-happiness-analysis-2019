# World Happiness Report (2019) - Exploratory Data Analysis

## Project Objective
The project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis on the **World Happiness Report 2019** dataset (published on Kaggle platform). The primary goal is to identify and quantify the impact of key macroeconomic and social drivers — such as GDP, social support, and life expectancy—on the overall happiness score of 156 countries. 

## Key Insights
* **Economic Prosperity as a Baseline:** There is a strong positive correlation between *GDP per capita* and the *Happiness Score*, confirming that economic stability is a primary driver of national well-being.
* **The Power of Social Safety Nets:** *Social support* emerged as the second most influential factor. Countries ranking highest in happiness share a common trait of strong community and institutional support networks.
* **Western Model Dominance:** The top 10 happiest countries are predominantly European (especially Nordic countries), suggesting a correlation between the Western socio-economic welfare model and high quality of life metrics.

## Tools
* **Language:** Python 3
* **Libraries:**
  * `pandas`: Data loading, cleaning, and manipulation.
  * `matplotlib` & `seaborn`: Data visualization and correlation mapping.

## Key Visualizations
* **Correlation Heatmap:** A matrix quantifying the relationships between all socio-economic variables.
* **Scatter Plots:** Visualizing the direct relationship between Wealth (GDP) and Happiness.
* **Bar Charts:** Highlighting the Top 10 happiest nations globally.

## Data Source
Data sourced from the Kaggle platform includes metrics such as:
`Score`, `GDP per capita`, `Social support`, `Healthy life expectancy`, `Freedom to make life choices`, `Generosity`, and `Perceptions of corruption`.
